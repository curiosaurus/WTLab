Temples

Here is a complete list of hundreds of temples in Nashik that you can visit during your stay in the city. To start with, check out Panchavati which is the place for anyone seeking some moments of tranquility. Panchavati in Nashik is a spiritual heaven for sure and you would feel rejuvenated to your core after spending your time at this holy place. Someshwar Temple on the Gangapur Road is another sacred site to pay your visit to. Bhakti Dham and Tapovan are two other ideal destination for seeking out a spiritual quest. Mhasoba Temple in Nehru Nagar on the Nashik-Pune highway is also a popular holy site in the region. The temple attracts thousands of devotees from across India throughout the year. Muktidham and Naroshankar temple are two more temples in Nashik that you can visit. Go through the list to find out which of these temples in Nashik you can see during your trip to the city.



KumbhMela

The Kumbh Mela is celebrated every six years at Haridwar and Allahabad and Maha Kumbh takes place every twelve years at four places in Allahabad, Haridwar, Ujjain, and Nashik. According to the Puranas, it is believed that Kumbh derives its name from an immortal pot of nectar, which the devtas (Gods) and demons fought over. The four places where the nectar fell are at the banks of river Godavari in Nashik, river Kshipra in Ujjain, river Ganges in Haridwar and at Triveni Sangam of Ganga, Yamuna and invisible Saraswati River in Allahabad.

Wine industry

Nashik has been described as "The Wine Capital of India" the Nashik region reportedly produced 10,000 tonnes of grapes per year. In 2013, there were 22 wineries in Nashik, out of 46 wineries throughout India. The largest vineyard in Nashik is the Sula Vineyards. In the harvest season, Nashik is home to several wine festivals, such as the India Grape Harvest and SulaFest.

Forts

Nashik district is on the west of sahyadri Mountains which made it an important place in medivel India It had various Forts on the hill tops as a great protection. they were constantly under attack from the Mughals and were strongly defended one such fort is Ramshej fort which was defended for 6 continous years of battle. The maratha empire in its earliest stages was only protected by these forts and provided a hideout to the citizens.



Nandur Madhyameshwar

Through ceaseless efforts of Bombay Natural History Society, World Wildlife Fund and Forest Department of the Government of Maharashtra, this sanctuary which houses more than 220 species of birds, 400 species of vegetation, 24 species of fish and several smaller mammals came into existence along the Nandur Madhmeshwar dam at the confluence of Godavari and Kadwa. Spread over the protected area of 10000 hectares, lies the core area of 1765 hectares, where the large variety of water birds, resident and migratory birds congregate in season. It is now counted as one of the important water fowl habitats in the region by the International Union of Conservation of Nature.
